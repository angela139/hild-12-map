HILD 12 Final Project

Scenario

For this map, we will be following a hypothetical Korean family (Kims) through generations of racial capitalism from Japan from 1910-1992

1910 - Beginning of Japanese occupation of Korea

Japanese began migrating to Korea and begin their take over. A Japanese agricultural company Taki Seihi Company, started by Taki Kumejiro, acquires land in the fertile lands of Honam Plains in Korea to grow his fertilizer business.

Kimje/Gimje Plantation Work

The Taki Seihi Company build their headquarters along with a large plantation at Kimje/Gimje. Some members of the Kim family begin working on their plantation. They are given low wages and face terrible working conditions due to the Japanese unchecked economic power in Korea.

Moving to Befu-cho

Taki Kumejiro uses his profits from the Kimje plantation to grow a fertilizer business in Befu-cho, Japan. The Kim family workers are forced to move to Befu-cho by the Taki Seihi Company where they continue to face difficult conditions. The Koreans are seen as inferior to the Japanese.

1932 - Beginning of Manchukuo

Manchukuo is a puppet state set up by Japan in Northeast China. The members of the Kim family that stayed in Korea move to Manchukuo to escape the poverty and horrible conditions in Korea from the Japanese takeover. They begin working in coal mines alongside Chinese workers, as the coal industry is a huge part of the Japanese energy industry. However, they continue to feel marginalized and excluded by the Koreans and Chinese.

1945 - end of Japanese control in Korea

Korea gains independence from Japan, but the Korean War ensues between North (communist) and South Korea (democratic). This severely damages both economies. Koreans in Manchukuo are given the region of Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture. The Kim family get settled in the region, living near the Tumen River which borders North Korea. They regularly see North Korean refugees who cross the river to escape the bad conditions in North Korea.

Late 1980s - early 1990s - Korean Wind

Some members of the Kim family migrate to South Korea to make more money amid the economic struggle in Yanbian. They send money back to their family members and hope to be reunited soon.
